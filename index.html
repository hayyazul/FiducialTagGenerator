ArUco and AprilTag Generator

Generate ready-to-print AprilTags and ArUco markers as PDFs. Free, opensource, browser-based, with built-in support for recursive nested tags.

About this generator

This is a simple web tool to generate PDFs of AprilTag and ArUco fiducial markers. Pick a family, the desired size, and tag IDs, and the tool packs as many tags as fit per page — with quiet zones and cut margins computed correctly. Everything runs in your browser. You can also download the github repository to run it offline.

What are AprilTags?

AprilTags are 2D visual fiducial markers developed at the University of Michigan’s APRIL Lab, widely used in robotics, drones, augmented reality, and computer vision research for pose estimation and object identification. The detector is open source and the bitmap encodings are published. This tool uses the official tag bitmaps from apriltag-imgs; the detector itself lives in AprilRobotics’ apriltag library.

What are ArUco markers?

ArUco is another popular family of 2D fiducial markers, originally developed at the Universidad de Córdoba and shipped with OpenCV’s aruco module. They differ from AprilTags in their bit layout and Hamming distances, but are similar in role. If your toolchain is OpenCV-based, ArUco is often the natural pick. See the OpenCV ArUco detection tutorial.

Supported tag families

  • AprilTag: tag36h11 (the robotics default), tagStandard41h12, tagStandard52h13, tagCustom48h12, plus the circular families tagCircle21h7 and tagCircle49h12.
  • ArUco: the DICT_4X4, DICT_5X5, DICT_6X6, and DICT_7X7 dictionaries at sizes 50, 100, 250, and 1000; the original ArUco dictionary; and the DICT_APRILTAG_* AprilTag-compatible variants.

Recursive nested tags

You can nest a smaller tag inside another tag’s center region. The tool computes the inner tag’s size and quiet zone automatically and renders both as a single printable PDF. Useful for multi-scale detection (close-range cameras read the small inner tag; long-range cameras read the outer tag) and for increasing detection accuracy as a whole. Currently supported on the tagCustom48h12 AprilTag family and on ArUco families with a sufficiently large center region.

Limelight, FRC, and printing notes

The PDF output is print-ready at the physical size you specify. Set your printer’s Page Scaling to None (sometimes called “Actual Size” or 100%) so the printed tags match the requested millimeter dimensions. For FRC (FIRST Robotics) competitions, tag36h11 at competition-spec sizes is supported.
